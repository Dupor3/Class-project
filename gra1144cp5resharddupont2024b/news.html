Linkin Park announce new co-vocalist for world tour seven years after death of Chester Bennington  

 Linkin Park recently announced that they’ll be hitting the road with a new co-vocalist, Emily Armstrong from the band Dead Sara, seven years after Chester Bennington’s passing. Armstrong brings her own powerful style, while Colin Brittain joins as their drummer. This updated lineup will perform on a 2024 world tour and release a new album titled From Zero, set to debut on November 15. The album’s lead single, “The Emptiness Machine,” was premiered at a livestreamed event in Los Angeles, giving fans a preview of the band's evolving sound and emotional depth. The tour is scheduled to stop in cities like Los Angeles, New York, Hamburg, London, Seoul, and Bogota. Many fans are celebrating Armstrong's addition, noting her unique style while honoring Bennington’s legacy, with the band stating that From Zero represents both their history and future as they navigate this new chapter. The tour kicks off on September 11 in Los Angeles and includes stops in major cities like New York, London, and Seoul, concluding in Bogota, Colombia. This announcement came after a cryptic online countdown that teased fans with hints of new beginnings, sparking anticipation for what’s to come. Reflecting on the album, Mike Shinoda emphasized that From Zero represents both a journey through the band’s past and a look toward the future. He expressed excitement over Armstrong’s addition and their commitment to evolving while respecting the legacy Bennington left behind.
